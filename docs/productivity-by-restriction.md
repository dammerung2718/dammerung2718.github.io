# Productivity by Restriction

> IDEA: Productivity of an individial is not enhancable, by its nature.
  One produces as much as his mind and body allows.
  But it is not immune to hinderance.

This premise may seem counter-intuitive, but in my experience it
is true.

If there are games to play, books to read or social media to scroll;
you are less likely to do anything else.  This is where your
productivity isn't at a normal level but is hindered by outside stimuli.

My solution for this was three rules.  Technology is allowed only if
it:

- can replace paper
- works with text
- embody an idea

Anything that can not comply to these three rules was strictly
forbidden.  Video conferencing, youtube, games, music (to some
extent), the font and code editor I use.  All were simply out of
the gate at these questions.

I left my gaming setup at bay, hid the books under my bed and removed
any app that doesn't work this way.  What remained was little, but
gave me more in return:

- Computer:
  * `coreutils`/`moreutils`/`tui`: Commonground for an environment.
  * `git`: Time & Space records.
  * `tmux`: Each workspace as a book.
  * `go`/`rust`: Language.
  * `helix`: Pen & Paper.
  * `fira-code`: Calligraphy.

- Phone:
  * `journal`: Diary.
  * `mail`: Mail.
  * `chatgpt`: AI.
  * `google-translate`: Translator.
  * `messaging apps`: WhatsApp/Telegram, friends/family use them so i need to.
  * `reddit`/`x`/`linkedin`: networking/information.
  * `banking apps`: money is hard man.

- Phone Dock
  * `contacts`: can call mama
  * `safari`: can read manga
  * `messages`: can read goverment ad campaigns
  * `spotify`: effectively my life

You will notice my computer has less. That is because I work with
that, not my phone. The phone also has a lot of stuff that is not
conforment with the rules but are a necessity such as banking apps
and social media for networking. I am not that addicted to social
media and networking is essential.  Social media is as addictive
as its notifications anyway, so I disable them all and continue
with life.

The dock is also different.  I need to be able to call people,
browse my usual manga websites and occasionally
[lobste.rs](https://lobste.rs). iMessage is conformant anyways.
The only exception is spotify. I love music. It is distracting.
What I found useful was to keep a single song on repeat while I
work.  If the place I work at is noisy or a library that distracts
you with silence (rather surprisingly) I put on my airpods, open up
something without lyrics and start to work.

Otherwise spotify is the most dangerous thing in this whole setup
that can hinder productivity for me. But since I am aware of it and
the enemy is disadvantageous in numbers (just a single app), it is
okay.


## Why Those Rules?

Because language is power.  It enables, creates, destroys and fosters
knowledge.  However brittle it is to change when faced with the
abundant lengths of time, the language you speak right now has never
been in a shape better than ever before.  You as an individual may
not have much impact, but ideas you keep hidden beneath are not
subject to those physical restraints.

They are as free as you are vocal, precise and articulate about
them.  Why do you think ads work? Why do you think people can lie
and decieve each other?  If it wasn't for their lack of power over
language, what else would we amount such damage to?

It is not our fault to be born inarticulate, or misunderstand each other.
Those HAPPEN, but it is our fault that we are not better than what we were before.

What is the common theme amongst all of these stories?
Gods BREAK THE LANGUAGE so the peasants stay peasants.
Language is a power such great that even gods are afraid of it.

If you can write what you have in mind clearly and understand what
is written down fully, then my friend there is no other person more
dangerous and powerful than you.  Let alone the peasants, you have
the ability to control yourself.  Your desires, fallacies and
weaknesses all succumb in the face of great articulation.

Heck if you do not believe me, believe him:

> If you can think and speak and write, you are absolutely deadly.
  Nothing can get in your way.

So that is what I did.
Forgo everything that is not language.
Forgo everything that is not human.
I wanted to be better.


## Conclusion (TL;DR)

Language is hard, life is hard.
Managing them together is almost impossible.
But it gets a bit easier each step.

Unhindered productivity is gained not by tools or processes, but
by removing every distraction on your way.
