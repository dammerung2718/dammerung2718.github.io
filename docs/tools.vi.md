# Vi

A simple text editor with convoluted controls.
The complexity it carries is a result of its history.
And yet because it is everywhere, we have to learn it regardless.


## Why

If possible, I use it in stead of others.
Not because I like hard to use tools or against IDEs or have deep hatred for WYSIWYG editors
or I am a dinosaur or I just like to torture myself.
Looking past all the arcane keybindings and stupid modal editing style, it is brutally simple.
There is text and a way to edit.
Nothing else.
**That** is what makes me want to use it.

The IDEs and WYSIWYG editors we have today are nice and often more useful than this simple, old, crippled text editor.
Yet the help they offer is confusing at times.
An entity between you and your thoughts is surely to ambiguate your intent.
It is its nature to do so.

In vi, there is no

- Colour
- Buttons
- File Tree
- Search Bar
- Language Server

Nothing between you and your thoughts.
It is you closest ever to the machine.


## Yes, but why?

I sometimes spiral into this deep state of brain fog.
Sometimes the document I have here is not in a nice font.
Or my IDE is not functioning the way I want it to.
Or the browser is using an ugly colorscheme.
Sometimes fate takes the better of me and my attetion swiftly slides away.
And I can't focus after that anymore.

At times like this, times where I search for a simple tool, there usually isn't one.
So if there is an opportunity, I appreciate and cherish simplicity.
